Verbal Bullying, and why it should be stopped

Around the world, one of the biggest problems has been bullying, with the most popular type of bullying being Verbal Bullying. As a part of this world, we should know how to prevent or stop this.

The first Federal Defenition of Bullying, released by the CDC, Is "any unwanted aggressive behavior(s) by another youth or group of youths, who are not siblings or current dating partners, that involves an is repeated multiple times or is highly likely to be repeated."

Verbal Bullying is one of the most common types of bullying. Verbal Bullying is when someone tells someone else something that hurts or insults them. Once you say something, you cannot take it back. Even if you say sorry, it could still leave permenant pain. This is why it is important to prevent bullying.

To prevent bullying, you must first undertsand the roles people play in bullying. The Bully is the person who bullies the other person. The Victim is the person being bullied. The Bystanders are people who are there to watch the bullying happen. A bystander could feed into the bullying, making it worse. They could also defend the victim by telling an adult or telling the bully to stop. Doing nothing at all is not good because it gives the bully an audience

One Way to Prevent Verbal Bullying from the bully's side is to think about what you say before you say it. Think: Is What I am saying OK? Will this hurt them? If the bully has a hard time stopping, they could talk to a trusted adult, such as a parent, guardian, counselor, or any adult you trust.

For the Victim, they could tell a trusted adult. One common thing people do is let a school counselor know, But you could really tell any trusted adult.

Bulllying is one of the worst things to do, and we can all do something to prevent it. Lets all work to fight bullying!
